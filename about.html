About Rare Resilience AI

Building the future of precision pediatric oncology

Our Mission

To transform outcomes for children with rare and lethal cancers through AI-powered digital twin technology that enables truly personalized, adaptive treatment.

Every year, approximately 1,000 children in the United States are diagnosed with lethal pediatric sarcomas — osteosarcoma, Ewing sarcoma, and rhabdomyosarcoma.

Despite decades of research, treatment protocols remain largely unchanged, and 30-40% of children don't respond to standard chemotherapy.

We believe every child deserves treatment optimized for their unique tumor biology.

The Problem We're Solving

Delayed Recognition of Treatment Failure

Oncologists must wait until surgery (week 12) to know if chemotherapy worked. By then, critical time is lost and the tumor may be inoperable.

One-Size-Fits-All Protocols

Despite molecular differences, all children receive the same treatment. We can't predict who will respond, leading to unnecessary toxicity and missed opportunities for better therapies.

Undetected Resistance

Resistance mutations emerge during treatment but aren't caught until clinical failure. Early detection could enable intervention before it's too late.

Late Relapse Detection

Recurrence is detected by imaging 6-12 months after molecular signals appear. Earlier detection could dramatically improve salvage therapy outcomes.

Our Approach

Dynamic Digital Twin

Unlike static prediction models, our digital twin continuously learns from each patient's response. As new data arrives every 3 weeks — imaging, ctDNA, biomarkers — predictions update and improve.

  • ✓ Baseline: Clinical + genomic prediction (72% accuracy)
  • ✓ Week 6: Multi-modal update (92% accuracy)
  • ✓ Real-time: Confidence grows from 60% → 95%

Multi-Modal Integration

We integrate 8 data types to create the most comprehensive picture of treatment response:

  • Clinical data (age, tumor characteristics)
  • Genomics (mutations, copy number)
  • Transcriptomics (gene expression)
  • Epigenomics (methylation patterns)
  • Serial imaging (CT/MRI/PET)
  • Circulating tumor DNA (ctDNA)
  • Blood biomarkers (ALP, LDH, CRP)
  • Treatment history and outcomes
