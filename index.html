The Challenge We're Solving

⏰

Too Late to Change Course

Treatment failures aren't detected until surgery at week 12. By then, critical time is lost and options are limited.

🎯

One-Size-Fits-All Treatment

Despite molecular differences, all children receive the same protocol. 30-40% don't respond, but we can't predict who.

🔬

Resistance Goes Undetected

Resistance mutations emerge during treatment, but aren't caught until clinical failure. Early detection could enable intervention.

📊

Relapse Detected Too Late

Imaging detects recurrence 6-12 months after molecular signals appear. Earlier detection could save lives.

Pediatric Cancers We're Targeting

Focusing on the most lethal childhood sarcomas

Osteosarcoma

~400 US cases/year
60% 5-year survival

Most common bone cancer in children. Standard treatment unchanged for 40 years despite molecular insights.

Status: Platform ready, clinical validation ongoing

Ewing Sarcoma

~200 US cases/year
70% 5-year survival (localized)

Aggressive bone and soft tissue cancer. Metastatic disease has <30% survival despite intensive chemotherapy.

Status: In development, launching 2026

Rhabdomyosarcoma

~350 US cases/year
68% 5-year survival

Most common soft tissue sarcoma in children. High-risk disease has poor outcomes despite therapy intensification.

Status: Research phase, launching 2027

Together, these cancers affect ~1,000 children in the US each year.

Our platform aims to improve survival rates, reduce treatment failures, and enable truly personalized medicine for every child.
