Privacy Policy

Effective Date: March 2, 2026

Last Updated: March 2, 2026

1. Introduction

Rare Resilience AI, Inc. ("Rare Resilience AI," "we," "our," or "us") is committed to protecting the privacy and security of your information. This Privacy Policy describes how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard information when you visit our website rare-resilience-ai.com (the "Site") or use our Dynamic Digital Twin platform for pediatric oncology (the "Platform").

This Privacy Policy applies to all information collected through our Site and Platform, as well as any related services, sales, marketing, or events.

IMPORTANT: We are deeply committed to protecting the privacy of children and patients. All Protected Health Information (PHI) and patient data is handled in strict accordance with HIPAA regulations. Please see our HIPAA Compliance Statement for details on how we protect health information.

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Personal Information You Provide

We may collect personal information that you voluntarily provide when you:

  • Register for our Platform or request a demo
  • Subscribe to our newsletter or communications
  • Contact us with inquiries
  • Participate in surveys or research
  • Apply for employment

This information may include:

  • Name, email address, phone number
  • Professional credentials and institutional affiliation
  • Job title and department
  • Communication preferences

2.2 Protected Health Information (PHI)

Healthcare Providers and Institutions: When using our Platform, healthcare providers may input patient data including:

  • Clinical information (age, diagnosis, tumor characteristics)
  • Genomic and molecular data
  • Imaging data and medical imaging results
  • Laboratory results (ctDNA, biomarkers)
  • Treatment history and outcomes

De-identification: All PHI is de-identified in accordance with HIPAA Safe Harbor or Expert Determination methods before being used for research, algorithm training, or platform improvement. We never use identifiable patient information without explicit authorization.

2.3 Automatically Collected Information

When you visit our Site, we automatically collect certain information about your device, including:

  • IP address and geolocation data
  • Browser type and version
  • Operating system
  • Pages visited and time spent
  • Referring website
  • Device identifiers

2.4 Cookies and Tracking Technologies

We use cookies, web beacons, and similar technologies to:

  • Maintain user sessions and preferences
  • Analyze website traffic and usage patterns
  • Improve user experience
  • Deliver targeted content

You can control cookies through your browser settings. However, disabling cookies may limit your ability to use certain features of our Site or Platform.

3. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect for the following purposes:

3.1 Platform Operations

  • Provide and maintain our Digital Twin platform
  • Process and analyze patient data to generate predictions
  • Improve our algorithms and AI models
  • Deliver personalized treatment recommendations
  • Generate clinical reports and documentation

3.2 Communication

  • Respond to your inquiries and support requests
  • Send administrative communications
  • Provide product updates and announcements
  • Send newsletters and educational content (with consent)

3.3 Research and Development

  • Conduct scientific research on pediatric cancers
  • Develop and improve our AI algorithms
  • Validate clinical predictions and outcomes
  • Publish de-identified research findings

3.4 Legal and Compliance

  • Comply with legal obligations (HIPAA, FDA, state laws)
  • Enforce our Terms of Service
  • Protect against fraud and security threats
  • Respond to legal requests and prevent harm

4. How We Share Your Information

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information. We may share information in the following limited circumstances:

4.1 Service Providers

We engage trusted third-party service providers to perform functions on our behalf, including:

  • Cloud hosting and data storage (AWS, Azure - HIPAA-compliant)
  • Data analytics and business intelligence
  • Customer support services
  • Email delivery and marketing automation

All service providers are contractually obligated to maintain confidentiality and comply with HIPAA Business Associate Agreements (BAAs).

4.2 Research Partners

We may share de-identified data with academic institutions, research organizations, or pharmaceutical companies for scientific research purposes. All data sharing is governed by:

  • Data Use Agreements (DUAs)
  • Institutional Review Board (IRB) approval
  • HIPAA compliance requirements

4.3 Legal Requirements

We may disclose information when required by law, including:

  • In response to subpoenas or court orders
  • To comply with regulatory inquiries (FDA, HHS)
  • To protect our rights or the safety of others
  • In connection with fraud investigations

4.4 Business Transfers

In the event of a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets, user information may be transferred. We will notify you via email and/or prominent notice on our Site before your information becomes subject to a different privacy policy.

5. Data Security

We implement industry-leading security measures to protect your information:

5.1 Technical Safeguards

  • End-to-end encryption (AES-256) for data in transit and at rest
  • Multi-factor authentication (MFA) for all user accounts
  • Regular security audits and penetration testing
  • Intrusion detection and prevention systems
  • Automated backup and disaster recovery

5.2 Administrative Safeguards

  • Role-based access controls (RBAC)
  • Employee security training and confidentiality agreements
  • Incident response and breach notification procedures
  • Regular security policy reviews

5.3 Physical Safeguards

  • SOC 2 Type II certified data centers
  • HIPAA-compliant cloud infrastructure
  • Restricted physical access to servers

Data Breach Notification: In the unlikely event of a data breach affecting PHI, we will notify affected individuals and relevant authorities within 60 days as required by HIPAA.

7. Children's Privacy

Our Platform is designed to serve pediatric oncology patients, but our Site is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13 without parental consent.

Patient Data: When our Platform processes health information for pediatric patients, all data is input by healthcare providers or authorized guardians in a HIPAA-compliant manner. We do not collect information directly from children.

If you believe we have inadvertently collected information from a child under 13, please contact us immediately at contact@rare-resilience-ai.com.
