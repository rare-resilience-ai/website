HIPAA Compliance Statement

Effective Date: March 2, 2026

Last Updated: March 2, 2026

Our Commitment to HIPAA Compliance

Rare Resilience AI, Inc. is committed to full compliance with the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of 1996 (HIPAA), as amended by the Health Information Technology for Economic and Clinical Health (HITECH) Act, and all related regulations including:

  • HIPAA Privacy Rule (45 CFR Part 160 and Part 164, Subparts A and E)
  • HIPAA Security Rule (45 CFR Part 160 and Part 164, Subparts A and C)
  • HIPAA Breach Notification Rule (45 CFR Part 164, Subpart D)
  • HIPAA Enforcement Rule (45 CFR Part 160, Subparts C, D, and E)

1. Business Associate Status

1.1 Our Role

Rare Resilience AI functions as a Business Associate under HIPAA when we process Protected Health Information (PHI) on behalf of Covered Entities (healthcare providers, hospitals, cancer centers).

As a Business Associate, we:

  • Execute Business Associate Agreements (BAAs) with all Covered Entities
  • Implement appropriate administrative, physical, and technical safeguards
  • Ensure that any subcontractors also comply with HIPAA (downstream BAAs)
  • Report breaches of unsecured PHI as required
  • Make PHI available to individuals upon request
  • Maintain and make available information for HHS compliance reviews

1.2 Business Associate Agreements

Before processing any PHI, we enter into HIPAA-compliant Business Associate Agreements that specify:

  • Permitted and required uses of PHI
  • Our obligations to safeguard PHI
  • Breach notification procedures
  • Termination provisions
  • Individual rights of access and amendment

To request a BAA, please contact: contact@rare-resilience-ai.com

2. Technical Safeguards

2.1 Encryption

Data in Transit:

  • TLS 1.3 encryption for all data transmission
  • End-to-end encryption between client and server
  • Encrypted API communications
  • VPN required for remote access

Data at Rest:

  • AES-256 encryption for all stored PHI
  • Encrypted database storage
  • Encrypted backup and disaster recovery systems
  • Full disk encryption on all servers

2.2 Access Controls

We implement robust access control mechanisms:

  • Unique User Identification: Each user has a unique identifier
  • Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA): Required for all accounts
  • Role-Based Access Control (RBAC): Users only access PHI necessary for their role
  • Automatic Logoff: Sessions timeout after 15 minutes of inactivity
  • Encryption and Decryption: Cryptographic keys protected and rotated regularly

2.3 Audit Controls

All PHI access and modifications are logged and monitored:

  • Comprehensive audit logs of all PHI access attempts
  • Tamper-proof, encrypted log storage
  • Regular log review and analysis
  • Automated anomaly detection and alerts
  • Logs retained for minimum 6 years

2.4 Integrity Controls

We protect PHI from improper alteration or destruction:

  • Data validation and verification mechanisms
  • Version control and change tracking
  • Digital signatures for data authentication
  • Regular data integrity checks

2.5 Transmission Security

PHI transmitted electronically is protected through:

  • Secure protocols (HTTPS, SFTP)
  • Message encryption
  • Network segmentation
  • Intrusion detection and prevention systems

3. Administrative Safeguards

3.1 Security Management Process

We maintain comprehensive policies and procedures:

  • Risk Assessment: Annual HIPAA risk assessments conducted
  • Risk Management: Documented risk mitigation strategies
  • Sanction Policy: Employee violations result in disciplinary action
  • Information System Activity Review: Regular audits of system logs

3.2 Workforce Security

All employees and contractors undergo:

  • Background checks before hiring
  • HIPAA training upon hire and annually
  • Confidentiality agreements
  • Termination procedures (immediate access revocation)

Minimum Necessary Principle: Workforce members only access PHI necessary for their job functions.

3.3 Security Awareness and Training

Our training program covers:

  • HIPAA Privacy and Security Rules
  • Password management and security
  • Phishing and social engineering prevention
  • Physical security protocols
  • Incident reporting procedures
  • Mobile device and email security

3.4 Incident Response

We maintain a comprehensive incident response plan:

  • 24/7 security monitoring
  • Designated incident response team
  • Documented response procedures
  • Regular incident response drills
  • Post-incident analysis and remediation

3.5 Contingency Planning

Our business continuity plan includes:

  • Data Backup: Automated daily backups, encrypted and geographically distributed
  • Disaster Recovery: Tested recovery procedures with <4 hour RTO
  • Emergency Mode Operation: Procedures to continue operations during emergencies
  • Testing: Annual disaster recovery testing

4. Physical Safeguards

4.1 Facility Access Controls

Our infrastructure is hosted in SOC 2 Type II certified data centers with:

  • 24/7 physical security personnel
  • Biometric access controls
  • Video surveillance
  • Visitor logs and escort requirements
  • Restricted access to areas containing PHI

4.2 Workstation and Device Security

We implement:

  • Locked screens when unattended
  • Encrypted hard drives
  • Mobile device management (MDM)
  • Remote wipe capabilities
  • Physical security cables

4.3 Media Disposal

All media containing PHI is securely destroyed:

  • Hard drives physically destroyed or degaussed
  • Certificates of destruction obtained
  • Secure shredding of paper records
  • Documented disposal procedures

5. Privacy Protections

5.1 Minimum Necessary Standard

We limit PHI use, disclosure, and requests to the minimum necessary to accomplish the intended purpose.

5.2 De-identification

For research and algorithm development, we de-identify PHI using:

  • Safe Harbor Method: Removal of 18 HIPAA identifiers
  • Expert Determination: Statistical verification by qualified experts

De-identified data is NOT subject to HIPAA restrictions and may be used for:

  • Algorithm training and validation
  • Scientific research and publication
  • Quality improvement
  • Aggregate reporting

5.3 Individual Rights

We support individual privacy rights including:

  • Right of Access: Provide access to PHI within 30 days of request
  • Right to Amend: Accept and act on amendment requests
  • Right to Accounting: Provide accounting of disclosures
  • Right to Restrict: Honor restriction requests when feasible

To exercise your HIPAA rights, contact your healthcare provider (the Covered Entity) or email us at contact@rare-resilience-ai.com with subject "HIPAA Privacy Rights."

6. Breach Notification

6.1 Breach Detection

We employ continuous monitoring to detect potential breaches:

  • Automated intrusion detection systems
  • Regular security audits and penetration testing
  • Employee reporting mechanisms
  • Third-party security assessments

6.2 Breach Response

In the event of a breach of unsecured PHI, we will:

  • Within 24 hours: Notify the Covered Entity (your healthcare provider)
  • Within 60 days: Covered Entity notifies affected individuals
  • As required: Report to HHS and media (if >500 individuals affected)
  • Document: Maintain records of all breaches for 6 years

6.3 Breach Prevention

We employ defense-in-depth strategies:

  • Multiple layers of security controls
  • Regular vulnerability assessments
  • Security patch management
  • Employee security awareness training
  • Third-party security audits

7. Subcontractor Management

Any subcontractor that accesses PHI must:

  • Sign a Business Associate Agreement
  • Demonstrate HIPAA compliance
  • Undergo security assessment
  • Provide proof of appropriate safeguards

Current subcontractors include:

  • Amazon Web Services (AWS) - HIPAA-eligible cloud services
  • Microsoft Azure - HIPAA-compliant infrastructure

8. Ongoing Compliance

8.1 Regular Audits

We conduct:

  • Annual HIPAA risk assessments
  • Quarterly internal security audits
  • Annual third-party penetration testing
  • SOC 2 Type II audits

8.2 Policy Updates

Our HIPAA policies and procedures are reviewed and updated:

  • Annually or more frequently as needed
  • When regulations change
  • After security incidents
  • When new technologies are implemented

8.3 Regulatory Cooperation

We cooperate fully with:

  • HHS Office for Civil Rights (OCR) investigations
  • State health authorities
  • Covered Entity compliance requests
  • Legal and regulatory inquiries

9. Patient Rights Under HIPAA

If you are a patient whose health information is processed through our Platform, you have the following rights:

Right to Access

You may request a copy of your health information. Contact your healthcare provider to submit an access request.

Right to Amend

You may request corrections to your health information if you believe it is inaccurate or incomplete.

Right to an Accounting of Disclosures

You may request a list of certain disclosures of your health information.

Right to Request Restrictions

You may request restrictions on how your health information is used or disclosed.

Right to Confidential Communications

You may request that we communicate with you in a specific way or at a specific location.

Right to a Paper Copy of This Notice

You may request a paper copy of this HIPAA Compliance Statement at any time.
